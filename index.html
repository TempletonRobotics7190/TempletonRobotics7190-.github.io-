About Us

In early November of 2017, a student by the name of Arsh Seth showed an interest in robotics. New to the school and country, Arsh set out to meet people that also shared his interests. Thanks to the supportive hand of one Mr. Warren Lloyd, a teacher at Templeton Secondary, Arsh was able to start an afterschool club. Between the budget cuts and insufficient funds, it was a rocky start from the get-go.

The club moved from room to room, finding a place to call their own. At the end of the search, we were left with an old and clustered storage space. Dedicated members spent their spare time cleaning and renovating the space. In under a month, we took damaged and soon-to-be dumped materials, and turned them into recycled goods. From a frameless chalkboard, to a busted computer frame, students, all with different types and calibers of skill contributed and made the Vancouver Rainstorms what it is today. Finally, with a place to meet and build, it wasn’t long until the team had come up with a design for their robot. Over a month of building later, the robot was completed and 7190 went to their first ever regional FRC competition, making it to finals and qualifying for the world championships in Houston Texas.
